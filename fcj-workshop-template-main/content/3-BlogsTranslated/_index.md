###  [Blog 1 - AWS Supply Chain Test Drive Simplifies Adoption to Explore Business Benefits](3.1-Blog1/)
This blog introduces AWS Supply Chain Test Drive—a solution that allows businesses to easily experience and evaluate the benefits of AWS Supply Chain before actual deployment. Test Drive provides a sandbox environment with sample data, enabling users to explore features such as inventory tracking, supply chain optimization, risk analysis, and demand forecasting. As a result, organizations can realize values like improved operational efficiency, cost reduction, better decision-making, and seamless integration with existing systems without large upfront investment.

###  [Blog 2 - Upgrading your AWS SDK for Go from V1 to V2 with Amazon Q Developer](3.2-Blog2/)
This blog guides you through upgrading the AWS SDK for Go from version V1 to V2 with the help of Amazon Q Developer. It highlights the benefits of SDK V2 such as better performance, modern APIs, support for more AWS services, and greater extensibility. Additionally, Amazon Q Developer helps automatically detect code that needs updating, suggests changes, and checks compatibility, making the migration process faster, less error-prone, and more efficient for developers.

###  [Blog 3 - Maximize business value with the AWS Supply Chain demand planning process](3.3-Blog3/)
This blog presents the demand planning process with AWS Supply Chain to maximize business value. It explains how AWS Supply Chain leverages historical data, AI/ML analytics, and forecasting models to help organizations accurately predict product demand, reduce excess inventory, minimize shortages, and optimize the supply chain. The blog also shares practical implementation steps, the benefits of applying this process, and recommendations for businesses to achieve optimal results in supply chain management.
